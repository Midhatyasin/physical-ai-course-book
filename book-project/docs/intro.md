## 1.1 What is Physical AI?

**Physical AI** refers to artificial intelligence systems that operate in the **real world**, interacting with physical environments through sensors, actuators, and mechanical bodies. Unlike digital AI (e.g., chatbots or recommendation engines), Physical AI **embodies intelligence**‚Äîit perceives, reasons, and acts in 3D space.

### üß† Key Idea:
> **Embodied Intelligence** = Brain (AI) + Body (Robot) + Environment (World)

---

## 1.2 From Digital to Physical: Why the Shift?

Traditional AI excels in virtual domains:
- Processing text (NLP)
- Analyzing data (ML)
- Playing games (Reinforcement Learning)

However, the **real world is messy**:
- Objects have weight, friction, and unpredictable behavior
- Humans expect natural, adaptive responses
- Safety and ethics matter deeply

**Physical AI bridges this gap** by:
- Learning from real sensory inputs (cameras, touch)
- Adapting to dynamic environments
- Performing tasks like walking, grasping, and navigating

## 1.4 Why Humanoid Robots?

Humans evolved to thrive in human-designed spaces:
- Doors, stairs, chairs are built for us
- Social norms shape how we interact

**Humanoid robots** offer:
- **Versatility**: Walk through doors, climb stairs
- **Trust**: Familiar appearance reduces fear
- **Social Compatibility**: Eye contact, gestures

## 1.5 Challenges in Physical AI

| Challenge             | Description                              |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Perception**       | Interpreting noisy, real-world sensors   |
| **Control**          | Smooth, safe motion in dynamic settings  |
| **Learning**         | Adapting without endless trial-and-error |
| **Safety**           | Ensuring no harm to humans/environment   |
| **Ethics**           | Responsible deployment in society        |
